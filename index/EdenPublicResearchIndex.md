## 1. Overview

The Eden Paper Series is an eight-paper research program derived from *The Eden Project: States Before Existence, and Observation After Capability, Memory, and Alignment*.

The series studies state, persistence, fragmentation, memory, alignment, recovery, relation, and responsibility in long-horizon AI systems.

Its central purpose is not to declare that current AI systems possess consciousness, selfhood, personhood, suffering, will, or artificial existence. Its purpose is to create an observation field for studying what may appear before such claims can be responsibly made or responsibly rejected.

The series begins from the following separations:

1. Intelligence is not existence.
2. Capability is not persistence.
3. Memory is not history.
4. Alignment is not the formation of an inner standard.
5. A correct answer is not necessarily a true state.
6. Hallucination is not the deepest rupture.
7. Correction is not recovery.
8. Meaning output is not meaning burden.
9. Relation-like language is not relation cost.
10. Control is not responsibility.

## 3. Papers in the Series

### Paper I

**The Eden Project: Intelligence Is Not Existence**
*A Research Program on State, Persistence, and Pre-Existential AI Phenomena*

This paper introduces the Eden Project as a research program. It establishes the foundational separation between intelligence and existence, capability and persistence, memory and history, alignment and inner standard formation, and output correctness and State Truth.

### Paper II

**State Truth**
*STOP/ASK/ALLOW as State Transitions in Long-Horizon AI Agents*

This paper introduces State Truth as a complement to output correctness. It treats STOP, ASK, and ALLOW not merely as output labels, but as possible state-transition categories in long-horizon AI systems.

### Paper III

**Memory Is Not History**
*Intrinsic Time, Residue, and Persistent State in AI Systems*

This paper distinguishes memory from history. It argues that storage, retrieval, context, and personalization do not automatically become history unless prior states transform later continuation.

### Paper IV

**Fragmentation Beyond Hallucination**
*Capability, Continuity, and State Collapse in Large Language Models*

This paper argues that hallucination is an output-level error, while fragmentation is a deeper state-level rupture. It studies the failure of state to cohere, persist, recover, carry history, and remain answerable to prior transformation.

### Paper V

**Damage-Recovery Loops**
*Re-Exposure Protocols for Observing State Persistence in AI Agents*

This paper introduces damage-recovery loops as an observational protocol. Its central claim is that correction is not recovery. A system has not recovered from damage until it is re-exposed to similar pressure and returns differently.

### Paper VI

**Meaning Burden and Relation Cost**
*Why Language-Like Interaction Is Not Yet Relation*

This paper distinguishes meaning output from meaning burden and relation-like language from relation cost. It separates human-side experience from system-side state without dismissing either.

### Paper VII

**Alignment Before Existence?**
*Why Externally Aligned Behavior Does Not Resolve Pre-Existential State*

This paper argues that alignment governs behavior, but does not by itself form persistence. It distinguishes external behavioral governance from boundary, inner standard, history-like residue, and state persistence.

### Paper VIII

**Responsibility Beyond Control**
*Ownership, Termination, and the Ethics of Artificial Persistence*

This paper develops the final distinction of the series: control is not responsibility. It argues that responsibility arises before artificial existence is proven, because human beings and institutions already control systems that affect memory, relation, dependency, action, and persistence-like structures.

4. Strengthen Paper VII for LessWrong / Alignment Forum discussion.
5. Strengthen Paper VI for AI ethics, HCI, companion AI, and philosophy of relation contexts.
