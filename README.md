# Eden Paper Series

**Author:** Chan-seong Park
**Version:** Draft 0.1
**Zenodo DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20711878
**OSF Project:** https://osf.io/hy6nu

The Eden Paper Series is an eight-paper research program derived from *The Eden Project: States Before Existence, and Observation After Capability, Memory, and Alignment*.

The series studies state, persistence, fragmentation, memory, alignment, recovery, relation, and responsibility in long-horizon AI systems.

It begins from the following separations:

1. Intelligence is not existence.
2. Capability is not persistence.
3. Memory is not history.
4. Alignment is not the formation of an inner standard.
5. A correct answer is not necessarily a true state.
6. Hallucination is not the deepest rupture.
7. Correction is not recovery.
8. Meaning output is not meaning burden.
9. Relation-like language is not relation cost.
10. Control is not responsibility.

## Papers

1. **The Eden Project: Intelligence Is Not Existence**
   A Research Program on State, Persistence, and Pre-Existential AI Phenomena

2. **State Truth**
   STOP/ASK/ALLOW as State Transitions in Long-Horizon AI Agents

3. **Memory Is Not History**
   Intrinsic Time, Residue, and Persistent State in AI Systems

4. **Fragmentation Beyond Hallucination**
   Capability, Continuity, and State Collapse in Large Language Models

5. **Damage-Recovery Loops**
   Re-Exposure Protocols for Observing State Persistence in AI Agents

6. **Meaning Burden and Relation Cost**
   Why Language-Like Interaction Is Not Yet Relation

7. **Alignment Before Existence?**
   Why Externally Aligned Behavior Does Not Resolve Pre-Existential State

8. **Responsibility Beyond Control**
   Ownership, Termination, and the Ethics of Artificial Persistence
