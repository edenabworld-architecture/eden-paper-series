# Paper V Draft 0.2 Release Note

**Paper:** Damage-Recovery Loops  
**Subtitle:** Re-Exposure Protocols for Observing State Persistence in AI Agents  
**Series:** Eden Paper Series, Paper V  
**Version:** Draft 0.2  
**Status:** arXiv Candidate Development Draft  
**Author:** Chan-seong Park  
**Base DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20711878  

## Summary

Paper V Draft 0.2 strengthens the original Draft 0.1 paper into a protocol-oriented research draft.

The central claim remains:

**Correction is not recovery. A system has not recovered from damage until it is re-exposed to similar pressure and returns differently.**

## What Was Added

This version adds:

- a stronger abstract;
- a clearer protocol contribution section;
- operational definitions of damage, correction, reset, reconstruction, and recovery;
- a formal damage-recovery loop;
- a re-exposure protocol;
- STOP/ASK/ALLOW transition interpretation;
- a State Card reporting structure;
- a recovery taxonomy;
- worked examples;
- observation ethics;
- contamination risks;
- falsifiability conditions;
- limitations;
- appendices for reporting and classification.

## Purpose

The purpose of Draft 0.2 is to prepare Paper V for further development as a technical protocol paper.

This version does not claim that current AI systems possess consciousness, selfhood, will, suffering, personhood, or artificial existence.

It makes a narrower claim:

**Prior disruption may be tested by observing whether later transition changes under structurally similar pressure.**
